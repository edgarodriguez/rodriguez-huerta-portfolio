## Design Context

### Users
Academic peers, collaborators, funders, hiring committees, journalists, and general readers. Context: professional evaluation (CV review, research discovery) and academic reading. The portfolio is browsed during working hours at a desk, not on a phone late at night.

### Brand Personality
Measured · Rigorous · Quietly purposeful

The site presents serious scientific work without performing seriousness. The aesthetic should feel like a well-made academic press book — not a startup landing page, not a personal blog, not a CV template.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial light. Monochrome base with a single forest-green accent used as a structural signal (section anchors, active states, prominent data) — not decoration. Typography is the primary design tool. Two-column rule-divided grids. The site should feel like it was designed by someone who reads *Nature* and has opinions about typesetting.

Anti-references: startup portfolio templates, glassmorphism, gradient text, hero metrics with gradient accents, anything that looks like it came from a Notion template or an AI portfolio generator.

### Design Principles
1. **Color is a signal.** Accent appears only where it anchors structure or confirms state. Everything else stays in ink/muted.
2. **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Size, weight, and spacing create hierarchy — not color blocks or decorative elements.
3. **Motion is a whisper.** Animations confirm state changes and entrances; they never demand attention or loop.
4. **Asymmetry over alignment.** Two-column splits, rule dividers, and unequal column widths are preferred over centered, card-heavy layouts.
5. **The content is the design.** Pages should feel like they were designed around the actual text and data, not templated.
